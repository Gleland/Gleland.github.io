Simple Bitcoin Tracker

Mon, Mar 5, 2018

Ever feel tempted to check that Bitcoin price again? Ever wanted to automate it so you can track prices yourself, or even use it for some analysis? Here is a very simple way of using Python to grab the price of BTC from blockchain.info.

As it turns out, there are quite a lot of fun things that blockhain can enable you to do without much effort. For this script however, we are going to keep it simple. Say you want the most recent market price of Bitcoin. Head over to blockchain's ticker, where you can convert the current price in BTC to ~20 different currencies. For this script, let's stick with USD.

The first thing we want to do is call the URL with python. Let's do that now:

                
                    from urllib.request import urlopen
                    link = "https://blockchain.info/ticker"
                    raw_data = urlopen(link)

Awesome! But now we want to convert it to a JSON structure (JavaScript Object Notation) for easier handling. And while we are at it, let's filter the USD parameter.

                
                    json_data = json.load(raw_data)
                    price = json_data["USD"]["last"]
                    print(f"${price}")

This last block of code will convert the data from blockchain's ticker, and convert the most recent price to USD.

And that's it! You've now taken your first step toward tracking bitcoin prices.
